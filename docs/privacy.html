Privacy policy

Last updated: March 9, 2026

Precog is an open-source Chrome extension. This policy explains how it handles your data.

What Precog accesses

Precog reads content from web pages you are viewing in Gmail, Asana, and Slack when you explicitly trigger it (via keyboard shortcut or context menu). This includes email subjects, message bodies, task descriptions, sender names, timestamps, and links.

Where your data goes

Your data is sent only to Claude (claude.ai) when you choose to generate and send a prompt. Precog does not send your data anywhere else. There are no analytics, telemetry, or third-party services.

What Precog stores

Precog stores your settings (email data scope, max title length, and custom block templates) using Chrome's built-in sync storage. No personal data or message content is stored.

Permissions

  • activeTab — allows Precog to read the current page when you activate it.
  • storage — allows Precog to save your settings.

Precog does not request broad host permissions and cannot access pages you have not activated it on.

Open source

Precog's source code is publicly available at github.com/morninj/precog. You can inspect exactly what the extension does.

Contact

For questions or concerns, open an issue at github.com/morninj/precog/issues.
